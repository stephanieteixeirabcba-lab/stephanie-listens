about me

Hi, I'm Stephanie.
(Or just S, if we're already friends.)

BCBA, Portuguese-American, mom, and someone who genuinely believes the best therapy starts with listening.

your photo here

The "official" version

I'm a Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) specializing in early intervention for children ages 0-5. I work with families across the USA and Portugal, offering services in both English and Portuguese.

I supervise BCBA candidates, train BTs and RBTs, and am launching CEUs and PDUs for behavior analysts in August 2026. I'm also an ACP through the IBAO.

The real version

I became a BCBA because I wanted to help.

I stayed because I saw what needed to change.

For too long, our field has prioritized:

  • Compliance over connection
  • Quiet hands over child voice
  • Data over dignity
  • Outcomes over experience

And while there's so much GOOD in behavior analysis — the science, the structure, the hope it gives families — there's so much that needs to evolve.

That's why I do this work the way I do.

That's why I show up here, every week.

That's why I'm building something different.

"Becoming a mom didn't just change my life — it changed my practice."

For years, I'd taught families how to handle big behaviors. I'd written behavior plans, collected data, and "intervened."

But it wasn't until I held my own child during their first big meltdown that I really, truly understood what parents had been telling me all along:

"It's harder than it looks. And no one prepared me for this."

That moment changed everything.

I stopped giving advice that sounded good on paper but didn't work in real life. I started building strategies that actually fit into a 7pm, post-tantrum, totally-spent kind of evening.

I started listening.

What I believe

  • Every behavior is communication. Even the loud ones. Especially the loud ones.
  • Every child deserves to be seen as a whole person. Not a diagnosis. Not a checklist. A human.
  • Every parent is doing their best with what they know. And what they know can grow — with the right support.
  • Every professional can grow toward more affirming practice. We don't have to be perfect. We just have to be willing.
